# NEON SPRAWL — The Dormant-City Model
### World Layer · Foundation Spec v1

The load-bearing wall of the world layer. The system that takes a fully-live city, **compresses** it into a lightweight summary, **drifts** that summary while you're away, and **expands** it back into a playable city on return. Everything else in the world layer (travel, a second city, rival-faction AI, the world map) builds on this. Get it solid in isolation first — even with one city saving/restoring to itself — before adding travel.

**Why foundation-first:** this is the highest-risk piece. If the compress→drift→expand cycle is wrong, travel and rival-AI rework on top of it. De-risk it alone.

---

## 1. The Core Cycle

```
   LIVE CITY (full ST sim)
        │  compress()   ← lossy: keep what carries meaning, drop the rest
        ▼
   DORMANT SUMMARY (a small object — numbers + named NPCs + history)
        │  driftDormant(elapsed)   ← the offscreen model advances it while you're away
        ▼
   DRIFTED SUMMARY
        │  expand()   ← regenerate a playable city: keep the named NPCs, repopulate the rest
        ▼
   LIVE CITY AGAIN (continuous-feeling, but not byte-identical)
```

The existing `serializeState`/`applyState` already proves a city can be frozen and restored perfectly. The dormant model is **deliberately lossy on top of that** — it throws away the anonymous background (regenerated on return) and keeps only what makes the city feel *continuous*.

---

## 2. What a Dormant City Remembers (the memory-depth decision)

**Decision: numbers + named key NPCs + a "what happened while away" log.** The reasoning:

- **Just numbers fails continuity.** If your recruited cell returns as "cellSize: 4" with no names, the people you won over are strangers again — that guts the emotional thread the city sim is built on. The Mayor you turned, the informant you flipped, the lead you recruited line-by-line — those *faces* are the point.
- **Full NPC persistence is the perf trap** the whole architecture exists to avoid.
- **So: persist only NPCs that carry narrative weight; regenerate the anonymous background.** ~5-15 named NPCs per city, not 50+.

### 2a. The Numbers (the city's vital signs)
A small flat object — cheap to store, the substrate the drift model operates on:
```
{
  // YOUR influence
  support, exposure, intel, cells, stance, doctrine,
  // THE REGIME
  grip, awareness, informantCount,
  // THE CITY
  prosperity, tier, population, income,
  // META
  faction: "oligarch" | "rival" | "independent",   // who controls it (for the world map)
  lastVisitTick, foundedTick,
}
```
These already exist in `ST.mov` / `ST.regime` / via `prosperity()` / `dayN()`. Compression = copy these out.

### 2b. The Named NPCs (the faces that persist)
Filter from the live pawns: **anyone who carries narrative weight.** Concretely, persist a pawn if:
- `p.recruited` (your cell — `cellRole`),
- `p.informant` (turned against you),
- `p.role` is mayor/enforcer (regime figures),
- `p._mayorTurning` / flipped (someone you turned),
- (later) named rivals.

Each persisted NPC keeps a **slim record** — not the full pawn:
```
{ id, name, role, allegiance, recruited, cellRole, informant,
  _mayorTurning, secretKnown, traits, relationshipToAvatar }
```
On expand, these become real pawns again (spawned into the regenerated city); everyone else is fresh population generated from `population`.

### 2c. The "While You Were Away" Log
A short list (cap ~6 entries) of what the drift model *did*, so changes are legible, not arbitrary. Generated by the drift step (§3): e.g. "The regime ran a crackdown — support fell," "Your cell pulled off a sabotage — the regime's grip weakened," "A rival faction began moving in." Read on return. **This is what makes a dormant city feel ALIVE rather than a save file with different numbers** — it turns the offscreen math into a story.

## 3. The Drift Model (the heart — a BLEND, tunable)

**Decision: a blend of three forces, with knobs for which dominates.** Pure entropy is punishing; pure momentum is toothless; pure volatility is chaos. The blend is the design; tuning picks the feel.

While a city is dormant, `driftDormant(summary, elapsedDays)` advances it by applying three forces each "tick" of elapsed time (e.g. per dormant day):

### Force 1 — ENTROPY (your absence costs you)
Your influence decays toward the regime's baseline; the regime recovers.
- `support` drifts down (rate scaled by how *little* infrastructure you left — a big cell decays slower).
- `grip` / `awareness` recover toward their natural set-point.
- Knob: `ENTROPY_RATE`.

### Force 2 — MOMENTUM (your work persists)
What you built keeps working, partially offsetting entropy.
- A standing **cell** keeps low-level agitating: it slows support decay and occasionally *generates* a positive event (a sabotage, a recruitment) — logged.
- **Seeds you planted** (`_planted` flags, high-allegiance sympathizers) can mature into support gains.
- Bigger/more-loyal cell → stronger momentum. Knob: `MOMENTUM_RATE`.

### Force 3 — VOLATILITY (the world moves on its own)
The city evolves by its own logic, independent of you.
- A chance per dormant period of an **autonomous event**: the regime cracks down (grip up, support down), a **rival faction makes a move** (faction can flip toward "rival"), an economic shift (prosperity swings), a random uprising or collapse.
- This is what makes return a *surprise*. Knob: `VOLATILITY_RATE` + an event table.

**The net drift** = entropy − momentum ± volatility, clamped, with every meaningful change writing a log entry. Default tuning leans mild-entropy + real-momentum-from-cells + occasional-volatility, so: *leaving costs you a little, but a cell you built genuinely holds the line, and sometimes you come back to a surprise.* All three rates are constants we tune in play.

## 4. Compress → Expand (the technical cycle)

### compress(ST) → summary
1. Copy the numbers (§2a) from `ST.mov`/`ST.regime`/`prosperity()`/population.
2. Filter + slim the named NPCs (§2b).
3. Snapshot `lastVisitTick`. Store the summary keyed by city id.
(The live `ST` is then free to be overwritten by another city — that's the state-swap, a later spec.)

### expand(summary) → live city
1. **Regenerate the city shell** — run the existing `genCity`/`genMap` (the city's physical layout). For v1 (one city returning to itself) the layout can be the *same*; for true multi-city, each city id seeds its own layout.
2. **Restore the numbers** into `ST.mov`/`ST.regime`.
3. **Spawn the named NPCs** as real pawns from their slim records (re-attach allegiance, recruited status, role, etc.).
4. **Generate fresh background population** up to `population`, minus the named ones.
5. **Surface the "while away" log** as a return briefing (a modal, like the day report).

**Continuity contract:** the player should feel "I'm back, and things moved" — NOT "this is a different city" (too lossy) and NOT "nothing changed" (no drift). The named NPCs + the numbers + the log carry the continuity; the regenerated background is acceptable churn (you don't track anonymous wisps' identities anyway).

---

## 5. Build Order (foundation-first, each validated)

1. **`compress(ST)`** — produce a summary from the live city. Validate: the summary captures the numbers + the right named NPCs.
2. **`expand(summary)`** — regenerate a playable city from a summary. Validate: numbers restore, named NPCs come back as real pawns, background repopulates, game runs.
3. **The round-trip** — compress then immediately expand (one city to itself). Validate: the city is *playable and continuous* (your cell is still your cell, support is intact). THIS is the core proof — if compress→expand round-trips cleanly, the foundation is solid.
4. **`driftDormant(summary, elapsed)`** — the blend model. Validate each force in isolation (entropy decays, momentum offsets, volatility fires events) + the log generates.
5. **The full cycle** — compress → drift(N days) → expand → play. Validate: returning after a simulated absence feels continuous AND changed, with a legible log.
6. **The return briefing** — the "while you were away" modal.

Each step validated headless before the next. The VISIBLE parts (the briefing modal) need in-browser confirmation.

---

## 6. Deliberately Deferred (NOT in this foundation)

- **Travel + a second city + the world map** — the *next* spec, built ON this foundation. This spec proves the model with one city round-tripping to itself.
- **City state-swapping** (two summaries, live-ST swap with the operative persisting) — risk #2 from the vision; its own design pass.
- **Rival-faction AI** (a faction actively running ops against you) — risk #3; its own pass. The drift model's "volatility" force is a *placeholder* for this (a rival "move" is just an event for now; real rival AI comes later).
- **Authored story spine** — hand-placed beats keyed to world state; later.
- **The operative running ops remotely in left-behind cities** — the open question from our design chat. Deferred deliberately: v1 is "you travel, the city drifts on its own (cell momentum is passive, not you actively directing)." Whether you can *remotely direct* a left-behind city is a future layer — and the dormant model is built to allow it (a left-behind cell's momentum could later take player orders).

---

## 7. The Open Question We Parked (for later resolution)

**"When you leave, what happens to your influence?"** — partially answered by the drift blend (§3): your influence *passively* persists (momentum) but *decays* (entropy) and the world *moves* (volatility). What's still open: whether you can **actively** keep directing a city you've left (remote ops). This spec deliberately builds the *passive* version first (cells agitate on their own) and leaves the door open for *active* remote control as a later layer — because the dormant summary already tracks your cell, giving remote orders something to operate on. We resolve "active remote influence" when we get to travel + multi-city, with real play to inform it.

---

## Bottom line

The dormant-city model = **compress (numbers + named faces + nothing else) → drift (entropy/momentum/volatility blend) → expand (regenerate, keep the faces) → a return briefing.** Built and proven in isolation (one city round-tripping to itself) before travel touches it. It's the foundation the entire world layer stands on, and it's designed so the city sim's emotional core (the faces you fought for) survives compression — which is the whole reason the world layer is worth building.
